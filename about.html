Budokai Jiu Jitsu

The Traditional Japanese Jiu Jitsu

WHO WE ARE Budokai Jiu-jitsu Dojo is a Martial Arts School dedicated to teaching Jiu Jitsu techniques while conserving the traditions and values of the Traditional Japanese Jiu-jitsu. Founded in 2016, Yeoville Budokai Jiu-Jitsu has successfully competed in provincial, national and international Jiu-jitsu trials and tournaments, recording hundreds of medals from all categories. We have developed a very exciting programme for high school and primary school to introduce them to the basic Jiu-Jitsu Skills.

OBJECTIVES OF BUDOKAI JJ DOJO

The long-term objectives of BJJD are far reaching and will have significant and important benefits for disadvantaged communities. The Dojo is building itself to become a non-profit organisation; and plans to open a sporting academy, which will provide the opportunity for children and adults to set their path towards a professional sport career.

  • BJJD is established for the following objectives:
  • Creation of an "Academy of Sporting Excellence".
  • Developing a “Budokai Trust Headquarters”.
  • Creating employment by equipping and empowering instructors to teach Jiu-jitsu as a profession.
  • Providing financial support to youth and children from disadvantaged backgrounds in order to allow them compete in national and international Jiu-jitsu event
  • Support the rehabilitation of "street children" and creating opportunities for underprivileged children to experience sport, including Jiu-jitsu.
  • Assist with the rehabilitation of youth against alcohol and drug addictions.
  • Preventing women and children abuse by training self-defence techniques to young girls and promoting the respect of gender equality by young boys.
